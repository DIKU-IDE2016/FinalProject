Cancer in the U.S.

In the United states, cancer still remains one of the leading causes of deaths. Ever been curious how the trends have changed over the years?


On this webpage, you will see a sequence of visualizations that will show you the trends in the development of the various cancers affecting men and women over the years. The data used are derived from SEER program and The American Cancer Society


To see the first visualizaion scroll down or click on the bouncing arrow below.

Leading Causes of Death in U.S, 1975 vs 2013

Discussion

The pie charts show the percentage of deaths by a particular cause(hover over sections to compare them), the percentage of death causes for all malignant cancer has grown significantly in time period from 1975 to 2013. It has risen from 19.3% to 22.6% - growth of 3.3%.

While these statistics are accurate, they can explained away as a decrease in percentages in other leading causes of death in the US.

In the next section, we take you through more detailed visualizations that show how cancer has progressed over these years and also how the various cancer types have progressed both in death and incident rates.

Discussion

In this section, we have been taking a closer look on what the percentage of malignant cancer really mean on the pie chart.

We see that the overall incidence rates of all cancers have increased over the years but even though there is an increase in 2013 compared to 1975, somewhere between 2000-2005 it started to decline. There is great news in the death cases, as this has been steadily declining since the year 2000.
